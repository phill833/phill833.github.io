They’re There By: Jacob Phillips “How’s the war, pal?” Doctor Yuelen asked Ennie with a warm pat on the shoulder. In a slightly forceful manner only an older person would assume would be alright because they were the last generation to expect a certain level of machismo to be necessary in order to properly raise boys into men. “What war?” Ennie says with a confused tone of voice. “Nothing, come on in, take your seat.” Yuelen beams. “But I don’t have a seat.” Ennie shivers in a juvenile manner. But. Butt. “Anywhere is good.” Ennie shoots Yuelen a glaring fucking you. Then Ennie smiles appreciatively. Then Ennie goes neutral as he imagines himself in the third person. Ennie does not have total control over his general character, his corporeal appearance, or really any of his actions in social situations. He is what some might call a ‘basket case’. Please, when I say this, understand that I don’t mean that Ennie is someone with a drinking habit he cannot manage or a gruesome drug addiction. I mean that Ennie literally loses control of his personality sometimes. For example, quite often Ennie will catch himself saying something very much unsavory to another person, being absolutely horrified about it, and being unable to stop the unchecked aggro. This behavior usually manifests itself as a bodily tremor during awkward turns of phrase in conversations. Ennie is always trying to love everyone he sees and get loved and liked simultaneously as if every encounter was all or nothing, forever. Clutching on(to) a one-way feeling, if you will. Ennie came from a well-educated family from the east coast and was struggling to establish his own identity after he left the D.C. area to learn and study at Isla Del Años. Ennie had the eclectic habit of collecting intricate stylistic components integral to other people’s personas. Ennie’s closet stands out as one mismatched collage paying homage to everyone he’s ever truly respected (from a visual standpoint), except for himself. His estranged brothers old combat boots, his grandfathers earthy flannel, a wannabe Ravenclaw blue sweater, faded black skinny jeans his skater friends always wore, and an oversized letterman jacket he had stolen as a final memento of his father. Ennie hated these meetings. He hadn’t really realized there was anything wrong with his personality yet. Or was at least at the age that any personality component comprising his identity was ‘part of who he was’ so ‘fuck you’ and all that. Ennie would spill some gut on weird childhood issues that sounded key but didn’t really matter like he’d seen in the movies and he’d leave and would be better for not have ever taken these meetings seriously. He sure as shit wasn’t going to. Say anything about the creeping paranoia he’d been experiencing for well over a month because first of all he’d get in trouble and second of all he was at about 60-40 in that his paranoia was well warranted. “I’m not doing so well, doctor Yuelen, I have… bats.” Ennie responded, with a sort of weird sexual undertone, which was another thing Ennie couldn’t control (vocal inflection). Ennie’s face was all screwed up. You could see a lot of wrinkles on his forehead and his eyes were squinted. He was wearing the face he made when he was fucked up high and thought people could read his mind. He felt like everything he had ever thought was somehow there for Nurse Yuelen to scrutinize and label and compartmentalize into boxes for every he has and has not met. “Oh. You’ve adopted pet bats?” Yuelen inquired with a fake smile. Like it was as normal as getting a dog. Which was actually pretty cool on his part. The main thing to do with unstable people is to go with the flow on their eccentric habits, so long as its safe and in good taste. “No, uhm. I have a colony of feral bats living in my duplex.” Ennie answered rather matter of factly, with a firm nod at the end. That was that. And so on. “Have you called animal control’? Yuelen asked while he took a protracted sip of fizzy chocolate milk from a can. “Yes, I called animal control. Some guy in a silver HAZMAT suit showed up at the crack of dawn. I was so relieved to see him I was like, Finally! My knight in shining armor. And he said no, sir, I’m just the batman, are you the one with the bat problem?” Ennie goes on. “So, I told him, Yes, take a look up the stairs. The animals have completely taken over the second story and are living in the rafters up above. Can you please get rid of them for me?” So, the batman started poking around the rafters and after about an hour he came down and told me that the bats are gray bats, which are an endangered species, so they’re not allowed to move them – the animals – and neither am I. He told me to just ignore them, and I’ve been trying to, but I just can’t. I mean, how am I supposed to ignore the repulsive little creatures? They have the most horribly disgusting hairy little hooked feet, and they make these screeching noises, like they are biting their bottom lips and sucking in air through their front teeth.” Doctor Yuelen moves his lips and makes the noise. “Oh, oh-my-god, do bats have lips?” Langley asks himself like it’s the end of the world. “I don’t know.” Doctor Yuelen says curtly in heavy monotone very much like he’d rather not think about the somewhat disturbing visual encompassed within the question therein. “I can’t concentrate; it’s all I think about - bats. And at night, with those beady little eyes peering down at you, it’s unnerving. I haven’t been sleeping. And it’s unsanitary. I’ve had to double my laundry hours just to get all the guano cleaned up. And the animals like only shit on my bed. Only the fucking bed. Can you believe that? My sheets get caked, I had to go out and find a store that sells black lights, because I support local businesses, and that took forever, and now I have to clean the sheets in the dark. I’ve even been ironing the sheets, because I think maybe the animals won’t shit on my bed if they realized the sheets are ironed and you know maybe they’ll have some shred of decency not to ruin them anymore. Does the chandelier (the chandelier is a somewhat affectionate term used to describe the powers that be who run the island) reimburse people for this type of thing?” Ennie says. “I’m sorry, I don’t know, Ennie. I can look that up for you if you give me a moment -.” Doctor Yuelen gets cut off. “And get this, the animal control guy called PETA, apparently the EPA “will punish me to the fullest extent of the law if I disturb the creatures in any way, shape, or form.” Can you believe that? It’s my duplex, not theirs. They didn’t pay for it. Why don’t they go get their own duplex? Or cave? Or duplex-cave? And as for myself, getting a new duplex is out of the question; I can’t just sell the place. Who would want to buy a place with bats in it? I mean, what would you do if there were bats in your…duplex?” Ennie asks rather exasperatedly and hopeful and begrudgingly. “I think that is something we need to talk about.” “I know that you’re a therapist and aren’t technically licensed to deal with bats but, I mean, well, you know, before we get to that, at first it was kind of cool. I’ll admit that, honestly. I felt like it was this new thing I had. Like, some people do yoga, others play video games, I wanted to be a batkeeper, you know? I constructed an insect farm, But then eventually it just got weird.” Ennie starts to continue. “Like embarrassing weird, you know?” “Ennie. I said we need to talk about something.” Yuelen presses. “Sure, sorry, what’s up?” Ennie asks. “Ennie, I’m afraid it’s happening again.” “Sorry?” Ennie seems confused as an ‘oh no’ begins to develop on his face. “Ennie, you’ve been telling the entire neighborhood that your home has a bat infestation. There are no bats inside your home. The safety responders have checked twice now.” Yuelen is calm and pensive and a little more imposing this time around. “That’s – that’s crazy – what are you talking about? You can’t go in my house. You went into my house? That’s breaking the law.” Ennie is worried and talking quick. “First of all, you can’t say crazy, you know that’s institutional policy we’ve adopted here, and we mean it when we say it. Its self-deprecative and inaccurate. And as far as privacy rights in relation to your home go, you forfeited that right when you signed the paperwork and moved to the Chandelier as a ward of our medical team here, we’ve been over this.” Ennie is beginning to look pale and nervous. “I’m sorry…none of us want this for you, it’s not your fault.” Yuelen takes off his glasses in a fit of tiredness as he strokes his temples. “We’re going to need to send you to Mellow House. It’s a good place.” Ennie’s entire body is shaking with nervousness. Then, without much warning, Ennie leaps out of his chair and does a surprisingly well executed somersault through a mostly opened glass window. Ennie is bailing as hard as he can from doctor Yuelen’s office. “Fuck that. I am never going back to any ward of any kind.” Ennie tells himself running with aerodynamic hands and long strides. This is understandable. Going to a psych ward of any kind makes Ennie feel like he’s losing some enormous battle concerning everything he’s ever loved. Doctor Yuelen calls the police. “Hello, this is doctor 457 calling in regarding patient 657111238911120. We have a runner.” “Roger that, doctor.” A safety responder affirms. For the record, safety responders had replaced trained policeman in affected areas as uber empathetic mental health workers with training in both healthcare and law enforcement practices. “Good for nothing male nurse.” Ennie says to himself before he finally stopped running once he reached the beach. Where he sat down for a moment to collect himself. Ennie was so heated and stressed out all the time that he had developed a strange allergic reaction to the sun whenever he got nervous. He began breathing real deep and heavy. “The vampire” Ennie smiles to himself. That’s what they call me. Nobody called Ennie the vampire. Anywhere. But if he had friends, that’s what they’d call him, he thought to himself. And if they didn’t, he’d bring it up and they’d all be all for it. Just to clear things up, Ennie had one bat, once, with the experienced stretched in order to have something to complain about. .
